Concept

Sounds Beyond Earth (SBE) is an audiovisual installation that lets visitors tune in to other worlds from the cockpit of a fictional spacecraft to the planet's ground. Select a celestial body and the interface jumps to it, allowing its vision from orbit or from its ground. Three channels open: Voice (press to speak and hear your words returned through realistic atmosphere + helmet/habitat emulation), Songs (choose a local track and reshape it into alien acoustics), and Signals (curated electromagnetic/plasma-wave recordings translated into audibility with a dial and live spectrum). SBE treats "astro sound" as mediation, not illusion: in space there is no sound; what we hear is what sensors, models and instruments allow us to hear. Built as an offline-first web installation (real-time 3D + WebAudio), it runs on a single screen with closed-back headphones and supports quick, repeatable interactions while rewarding deeper listening for both individual and shared public encounters.

How It Works

VOICE

Press-to-speak listening loop. Your voice is remapped with atmospheric models and helmet or habitat emulation for each selected world.

SONGS

Load a local track and shift timbre, diffusion, and resonances to hear how familiar music mutates under extraterrestrial acoustic constraints.

SIGNALS

Explore curated electromagnetic and plasma-wave recordings translated into audibility with a tuning dial and live spectrum feedback.

Tech + Exhibit

Designed for focused, headphones-only listening, SBE runs as an offline-first web installation combining real-time 3D rendering and WebAudio processing. The setup is optimized for high-throughput public exhibitions while preserving depth for longer sessions.

  • Listening mode: Headphones only (closed-back recommended).
  • Runtime: Offline-first web install (real-time 3D + WebAudio).
  • Footprint: Approx. 2.5m x 2m area with one screen and one listener position.
  • Required equipment: 1 computer, 1 display/projector, 1 pair of closed-back headphones, stable power, optional operator station.
